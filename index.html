a tribute to Markus Brunetti

When a new technology meets her artist,
a fascinating story unveils before contemporaneous eyes.

Since the 2014 Makk’s exhibition, in Köln (Germany), Markus Brunetti’s body of work spreads the world and fascinates viewers: digital photography met art in the subtle form of a new sensitivity. Churches, cathedrals, european architectural chefs d’œuvre reveals the details of their mastery and craftsmanship through the original work of an artist. From hundreds of clichés, Markus Brunetti composes flattened, detail rich photographs of facades. This patient workflow reveals the monumental power of the volumes drawn in the sand by architects, and the minute details of sculptures and ornaments. Azulejos!

Free from modern cities disturbances (cars, visitors, antennas, lights…) those gigantic prints take the viewer out of time and space, immersed in a glorious two dimensional experiment. Thus extending in our modern environments one very purpose of these monuments, assemble the best of human crafts to defy the injuries of centuries.

To discover more of the fascinating prints of Markus Brunetti’s, you may follow the links on this search page or visit his official website. An ongoing and travelling body of work will be brought to you right from the Internet. Digital mastery at its very best.
As photography needs a print validation, never hesitate to enter galleries and museums exhibiting Markus Brunetti’s works. The actual confrontation to these pictures is moving and impressive, unforgettable: « vaut le détour !».
